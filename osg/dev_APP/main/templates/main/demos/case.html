THE SURVEY

Undersigned boarded the vessel on October 7th at 14:00 hrs LT in order to survey and determine general conditions of the vessel’s grabs.

According to the vessel’s Master and C/O during the loading operations at Batiliman Port, Turkey, the hoisting wire of crane number 4 broke due to rough practice of the stevedores which caused that the grab No. 4 to fall down to the shore and some parts of the grabs were allegedly broken.

During the survey, the condition of all grabs was inspected in detail, compared between each other, and also checked if the damages occurred in grab #4 was done because of the mentioned incident at the loading port. Photos of all grabs can be seen in the following pages:

Chief Officer informed that the hoisting wire jumped from the sheave to the shaft and this caused the wire to break off after. Nevertheless, undersigned observed that the shaft has excessively worn which indicates the possibility that wire was running on the shaft and not on the sheave for a long time before the incident happened, and friction caused the breakage of wire and not stevedores mishandling.

GENERAL COMMENTS ON INSPECTION

From the above photos, all four grabs found to be highly deteriorated, they were heavily rusted with very thick rust flakes and corrosion. Moreover, in grabs number 2 and 3 we could see evident deformations of the bucket’s steel.

Grabs number 1, 2 and 4 have broken frames and plates since they have cracks from 5 to 25 centimeters which indicates that they were in very poor condition much before the incident at the loading port.

Grab number 2 has a very similar crack, in the same location as one of the claimed damages of grab 4 as seen below:

Revising the report done by SAM MARINE at loading port, after the grab was damaged, we could extract the below photo:

The above photo from SAM MARINE’s report shows that the surface of the crack in grab number 4 has two colors (blue area). Silver color which indicates a fresh cut, and orange color which indicates that the crack was there before the grab was used and damaged. Most probably the original crack extended (green arrow) after the grab fell down when the wire was broken.

Undersigned requested all grabs certificates of maintenance records and the vessel was not able to provide them. They could only find two load test certificates dated from August 25th, 2015 (RINA) and from February 17th, 2012 (LLOYDS).

Undersigned also asked the Master and C/O about the possibility to repair the Grab No. 4 during the voyage to Ghana and they informed that they did not have the material, equipment and certified fitter to do the repairs. The vessel prefers a person from a shore company that performs the job.

We were also informed that the vessel does not have a spare grab on board.

According to our findings, we should comment that the grabs were in very poor conditions and unsafe to be used during cargo operations.
